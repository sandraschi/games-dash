Basic Gameplay

Blackjack (also called 21) is a card game where you compete against the dealer. The goal is to get a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer without going over.

Card Values

  • Number cards (2-10): Face value
  • Face cards (J, Q, K): Worth 10
  • Ace: Worth 1 or 11 (whichever is better for your hand)

How to Play

  1. Place your bet
  2. Dealer deals two cards to you and two to themselves (one dealer card is hidden)
  3. If you get 21 (Ace + 10-value card), you have "Blackjack" and win 1.5x your bet (unless dealer also has Blackjack)
  4. Choose your action:
    • Hit: Take another card
    • Stand: Keep your current hand
    • Double Down: Double your bet and take exactly one more card (only on first two cards)
  5. If you go over 21, you "bust" and lose immediately
  6. After you stand, dealer reveals their hidden card and must hit until they reach 17 or higher
  7. Compare hands - closest to 21 without going over wins

Winning Conditions

  • Blackjack (21 with first two cards): Pays 3:2 (1.5x bet)
  • Beat dealer's hand: Pays 1:1 (double your bet)
  • Tie (Push): Your bet is returned
  • Dealer busts: You win even if you busted

üéØ Strategy

Basic Strategy (The Foundation)

Blackjack has mathematically optimal play called "basic strategy." Following it reduces the house edge to about 0.5%.

When to Hit

  • Hand value 5-11: Always hit
  • Hand value 12-16: Hit if dealer shows 7 or higher
  • Soft hands (Ace + low card): More flexible - can hit without risk of busting

When to Stand

  • Hand value 17-21: Always stand
  • Hand value 12-16: Stand if dealer shows 2-6 (dealer likely to bust)

When to Double Down

  • Hand value 9, 10, or 11: Double if dealer shows 2-9
  • Soft 13-18: Double if dealer shows 4-6
  • Only double on your first two cards

Advanced Concepts

  • Hard hand: No Ace, or Ace counts as 1
  • Soft hand: Ace counts as 11 (can't bust on next card)
  • Card counting: Advanced technique (not applicable in online games with continuous shuffling)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Taking insurance (bad bet - 5.8% house edge)
  • Standing on 12-16 when dealer shows 7-Ace
  • Not doubling down on 10 or 11
  • Playing by "gut feeling" instead of basic strategy

üìö History

Origins

Blackjack's origins are debated, but it likely evolved from several European card games in the 17th-18th centuries. The Spanish game "Veintiuna" (21) and French "Vingt-et-Un" (21) are early ancestors.

Name Origin

The name "Blackjack" comes from a special bonus payout in early American casinos. If a player got a black Jack (Jack of Spades or Clubs) with an Ace of Spades, they received a 10:1 bonus. This bonus was later removed, but the name stuck.

Modern Blackjack

Blackjack became the most popular casino card game in the 20th century, especially after:

  • 1962: Edward Thorp published "Beat the Dealer" introducing card counting
  • 1970s: Basic strategy became widely known
  • 1990s: Online blackjack made the game accessible worldwide

Cultural Impact

Blackjack is featured in countless movies (Rain Man, 21, The Hangover) and is the casino game most associated with skill and strategy. The MIT Blackjack Team's story became legendary.

History

Ticket to Ride was designed by Alan R. Moon and first published in 2004 by Days of Wonder. The game was inspired by Moon's love of train travel and his desire to create an accessible gateway game that would introduce new players to modern board gaming.

Inception

Alan R. Moon had been designing games since the 1980s, but Ticket to Ride was his breakthrough hit. The game was inspired by his travels across the United States by train and his fascination with the golden age of rail travel. Moon wanted to create a game that was:

  • Easy to learn (gateway game)
  • Quick to play (30-60 minutes)
  • Visually appealing
  • Strategic but not overwhelming

Gateway Game

Ticket to Ride is considered one of the most successful gateway games - games that introduce new players to modern board gaming. It bridges the gap between traditional family games (like Monopoly) and more complex strategy games. The game's success helped popularize modern board gaming and introduced millions of players to the hobby.

Awards & Recognition

  • 2004: Winner of Spiel des Jahres (Game of the Year) - the most prestigious board game award
  • 2004: Winner of Origins Award for Best Board Game
  • 2005: Winner of Diana Jones Award for Excellence in Gaming
  • Over 8 million copies sold worldwide
  • Translated into 25+ languages

Game Rules

Objective

The goal is to score the most points by:

  1. Claiming routes between cities (points based on route length)
  2. Completing destination tickets (bonus points for connecting two cities)
  3. Building the longest continuous path (10 bonus points)

Setup

  • Each player receives 45 colored train pieces and 4 train cards
  • Each player draws 3 destination tickets and must keep at least 2
  • Place 5 train cards face-up on the table
  • The rest form a draw pile

Gameplay

On your turn, you must do one of the following:

  1. Draw Train Cards:
    • Draw 2 cards from the face-up cards (if you take a wild card, that's your only card)
    • OR draw 2 cards from the face-down deck
    • You can mix face-up and face-down cards
    • If you take a face-up card, it's immediately replaced
  2. Claim a Route:
    • Select a route between two adjacent cities
    • Play train cards matching the route's color and length
    • Place your colored trains on the route
    • Score points based on route length
    • Some routes are double routes (two parallel routes) - only one player can claim each
  3. Draw Destination Tickets:
    • Draw 3 destination tickets
    • Keep at least 1 (you can keep all 3)
    • Return unwanted tickets to the bottom of the deck
    • You can only do this once per game

Route Colors

Routes come in different colors:

  • Colored routes: Require matching train cards (red, blue, green, yellow, orange, pink, white, black)
  • Gray routes: Can be claimed with any color train cards
  • Wild cards (locomotives): Can be used as any color

Scoring

Route Length Points
1 train 1 point
2 trains 2 points
3 trains 4 points
4 trains 7 points
5 trains 10 points
6 trains 15 points

Destination Tickets:

  • If completed: Add the ticket's point value to your score
  • If incomplete: Subtract the ticket's point value from your score
  • Ticket values range from 4 to 22 points

Longest Route Bonus:

  • The player with the longest continuous path of trains scores 10 bonus points
  • In case of a tie, all tied players receive the bonus

End of Game

The game ends when a player has 2 or fewer trains remaining. After that player finishes their turn, all players get one more turn. Then:

  1. Players reveal their destination tickets
  2. Score completed tickets (add points) and incomplete tickets (subtract points)
  3. Determine longest route (10 bonus points)
  4. Highest score wins!

Strategy & Tips

Basic Strategy

  • Balance route claiming and card collection: Don't claim routes too early - build up cards first
  • Focus on destination tickets: Complete your tickets, but don't take too many risky ones
  • Block opponents: Claim routes that opponents need for their tickets
  • Watch the train count: Don't let the game end when you're not ready

Destination Tickets

  • Keep tickets that overlap: If multiple tickets share cities, you can complete them together
  • Evaluate risk vs. reward: High-point tickets are tempting but risky if incomplete
  • Don't take too many: Each incomplete ticket hurts your score
  • Plan your route: Think about the path you'll need to complete tickets

Route Claiming

  • Claim double routes early: If you need a route, claim it before opponents block you
  • Save wild cards: Use them for long routes or when you're short on a color
  • Watch for bottlenecks: Some cities are critical connection points
  • Claim gray routes strategically: They're flexible but can be blocked by any color

Card Management

  • Collect sets of colors: Focus on 2-3 colors rather than spreading out
  • Watch face-up cards: Take cards opponents need to block them
  • Don't hoard too many: You can only hold 7 cards (in some variants)
  • Time your claims: Claim routes when you have enough cards, not too early

End Game

  • Control the end: If you're ahead, try to end the game quickly
  • Complete tickets: Make sure you finish your destination tickets before the game ends
  • Longest route: If you're close, try to claim the longest route bonus
  • Count trains: Keep track of how many trains each player has left

Cultural Impact

Ticket to Ride has had a significant impact on modern board gaming:

  • Gateway Game Success: Introduced millions of players to modern board gaming
  • Industry Growth: Helped fuel the board game renaissance of the 2000s
  • Digital Versions: Available on iOS, Android, Steam, Xbox, and online platforms
  • Educational Use: Used in schools to teach geography and strategy
  • Competitive Scene: Has organized tournaments and championships
  • Influence: Inspired many other route-building and connection games

Tips for Beginners

  • Start simple: Focus on completing 2-3 destination tickets rather than many
  • Collect cards first: Build up a hand of train cards before claiming routes
  • Watch opponents: See what routes they're collecting cards for
  • Plan ahead: Think about which routes you'll need for your tickets
  • Don't hoard: Claim routes when you can - don't wait too long
  • Block strategically: If an opponent needs a route, consider claiming it first
  • Balance risk: High-point tickets are great, but incomplete ones hurt
  • Have fun: Ticket to Ride is meant to be accessible and enjoyable!

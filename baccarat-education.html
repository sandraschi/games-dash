Basic Gameplay

Baccarat is a card game where you bet on which hand will have a value closer to 9: Player or Banker. You can also bet on a Tie.

Card Values

  • Ace: Worth 1
  • 2-9: Face value
  • 10, J, Q, K: Worth 0

Important: Hand values use modulo 10 - if total exceeds 9, drop the tens digit (e.g., 15 becomes 5).

How to Play

  1. Place your bet on Player, Banker, or Tie
  2. Dealer deals two cards to Player and two to Banker
  3. If either hand totals 8 or 9 (natural), no more cards are drawn
  4. Otherwise, third card rules apply:
    • Player: Draws third card if hand value is 0-5
    • Banker: Draws third card based on complex rules (depends on Player's third card)
  5. Hand closest to 9 wins
  6. If both hands are equal, Tie bets win

Betting Options

  • Player: Pays 1:1 (even money)
  • Banker: Pays 0.95:1 (5% commission - best bet!)
  • Tie: Pays 8:1 (or 9:1 in some casinos)

Third Card Rules (Simplified)

Player draws if: Hand value 0-5

Banker draws if:

  • Banker 0-2: Always draws
  • Banker 3: Draws unless Player's third card is 8
  • Banker 4: Draws if Player's third card is 2-7
  • Banker 5: Draws if Player's third card is 4-7
  • Banker 6: Draws if Player's third card is 6-7
  • Banker 7: Stands (no third card)

üéØ Strategy

House Edge

  • Banker bet: 1.06% house edge (BEST BET!)
  • Player bet: 1.24% house edge
  • Tie bet: 14.4% house edge (AVOID!)

Best Strategy

Always bet on Banker! It has the lowest house edge of any casino game bet (except craps odds bets).

  • Banker wins slightly more often than Player (due to drawing rules)
  • The 5% commission is still better than Player's 1.24% edge
  • This is the mathematically optimal strategy

Betting Systems

Like roulette, betting systems don't work in baccarat. However, since Banker has such a low house edge, some players use conservative progression systems. Still, no system can overcome the house edge long-term.

Common Mistakes

  • Betting on Tie (terrible odds - 14.4% house edge)
  • Betting on Player instead of Banker
  • Following "patterns" - each hand is independent
  • Not accounting for the 5% commission on Banker wins

Smart Play

  • Always bet Banker (lowest house edge)
  • Never bet Tie (terrible odds)
  • Set a budget and stick to it
  • Remember: Each hand is independent - no patterns exist
  • Enjoy the simplicity - baccarat is one of the easiest casino games to play

üìö History

Origins

Baccarat's origins are unclear, but it likely evolved from Italian card games in the 15th century. The name comes from the Italian "baccara" meaning "zero" (referring to face cards being worth zero).

European Popularity

Baccarat became popular in France in the 19th century, especially among the aristocracy. It was known as "Chemin de Fer" (railway) in France and "Punto Banco" in other European countries.

Casino Evolution

  • 1950s: Baccarat was introduced to Las Vegas casinos
  • 1960s-1980s: Became the game of choice for high rollers
  • 1990s: Mini-baccarat tables made it accessible to regular players
  • 2000s: Online baccarat exploded in popularity, especially in Asia

Fill in the words using clues

üìú The Rules

Objective: Fill in all the white squares with letters to form words that match the given clues.

How to Play

  • Read the clues (Across and Down)
  • Click on a square to select it
  • Type letters to fill in words
  • Words must intersect correctly (shared letters must match)
  • Complete all words to solve the puzzle!

Clue Types

  • Direct clues: Straightforward definitions
  • Wordplay: Puns, anagrams, homophones
  • Cryptic: Complex wordplay (advanced puzzles)
  • Themed: Related to a specific topic

üéØ Strategy Tips

Solving Techniques

  • Start with fill-in-the-blanks: Easiest clues to solve
  • Use short words: 3-4 letter words are often common (THE, AND, ARE)
  • Look for patterns: Common letter combinations (TH, ER, ING)
  • Work intersections: Use crossing letters to narrow down possibilities

Advanced Strategies

  • Theme awareness: Themed puzzles have related answers
  • Word length: Count squares to know word length
  • Common endings: -ING, -ED, -ER, -ION are frequent
  • Vowel placement: Every word needs vowels - use this to eliminate options

Puzzle Types

  • American style: More black squares, fewer words
  • British style: More words, fewer black squares, more interlocking
  • Cryptic: Complex wordplay, requires special techniques

üìö History

Crossword puzzles were invented by Arthur Wynne and first published in the New York World newspaper on December 21, 1913. The first puzzle was diamond-shaped.

Popularity

Crosswords became a worldwide phenomenon. The New York Times crossword, started in 1942, is considered the gold standard. Crosswords appear in newspapers, magazines, and books globally.

Cultural Impact

Crosswords have influenced language, popularized obscure words, and created a subculture of dedicated solvers. They're used for education, entertainment, and even cognitive health benefits.

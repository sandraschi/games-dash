üìñ Rules

Basic Gameplay

Craps is a dice game where players bet on the outcome of dice rolls. The game has two main phases:

Come-Out Roll (First Roll)

  • Natural (7 or 11): Player wins immediately
  • Craps (2, 3, or 12): Player loses immediately
  • Point (4, 5, 6, 8, 9, or 10): This number becomes the "point" and the game enters the Point Phase

Point Phase

  • Player continues rolling until either:
  • Point is rolled again: Player wins
  • 7 is rolled: Player loses (this is called "sevening out")
  • Any other number: Continue rolling

Betting Options

  • Pass Line: Bet that the shooter will win (most common bet)
  • Don't Pass: Bet that the shooter will lose
  • Come Bet: Similar to Pass Line, but made after the point is established
  • Don't Come: Opposite of Come Bet
  • Odds Bet: Additional bet on the point with no house edge (best bet in casino)

üéØ Strategy

Best Bets

  • Pass Line + Odds: Lowest house edge (0.8% on Pass Line, 0% on Odds)
  • Don't Pass + Odds: Even lower house edge (0.7% on Don't Pass)
  • Come + Odds: Same as Pass Line, can be made multiple times

Bets to Avoid

  • Any 7: High house edge (16.67%)
  • Hard Ways: Very high house edge (9-11%)
  • Proposition Bets: Single-roll bets with very high house edge

Basic Strategy

  1. Start with Pass Line or Don't Pass bet
  2. Once point is established, take maximum odds (if available)
  3. Consider Come bets for additional action
  4. Avoid proposition bets and hard ways
  5. Set a budget and stick to it

üìö History

Origins

Craps evolved from the English game "Hazard" and was brought to America by French settlers. The name "craps" comes from "crabs," the French term for the losing roll of 2.

Modern Craps

Modern casino craps was developed in the early 20th century. The game became popular in Las Vegas and is now one of the most exciting casino games, known for its fast pace and social atmosphere.

Cultural Impact

Craps is featured in many movies and TV shows, often associated with high-stakes gambling and casino culture. The game's unique terminology and rituals (like calling out numbers) make it distinctive.

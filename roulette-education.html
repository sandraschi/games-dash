üìñ Rules

Basic Gameplay

Roulette is a casino game where players bet on where a ball will land on a spinning wheel. The wheel has numbered pockets (0-36 in European Roulette).

Wheel Layout

  • European Roulette: 37 numbers (0, 1-36)
  • 0 is green (house advantage)
  • 1-36 alternate red and black (except 0)
  • Numbers are arranged in a specific order (not sequential)

Betting Options

Inside Bets (Specific Numbers)

  • Straight Up: Bet on a single number (35:1 payout)
  • Split: Bet on two adjacent numbers (17:1)
  • Street: Bet on three numbers in a row (11:1)
  • Corner: Bet on four numbers (8:1)
  • Six Line: Bet on six numbers (5:1)

Outside Bets (Groups of Numbers)

  • Red/Black: Bet on color (1:1 payout)
  • Odd/Even: Bet on odd or even numbers (1:1)
  • 1-18 / 19-36: Low or high numbers (1:1)
  • Dozens: 1-12, 13-24, or 25-36 (2:1)
  • Columns: One of three vertical columns (2:1)

How to Play

  1. Place your bet on the betting area
  2. Click "Spin" to spin the wheel
  3. Ball lands in a numbered pocket
  4. Winning bets are paid according to odds
  5. Losing bets are collected by the house

üéØ Strategy

House Edge

European Roulette has a house edge of 2.7% (one zero). This means over time, the casino keeps 2.7% of all bets.

Best Bets (Lowest House Edge)

  • Outside bets (Red/Black, Odd/Even, 1-18/19-36): 1:1 payout, 2.7% house edge
  • Dozens/Columns: 2:1 payout, 2.7% house edge
  • All bets have the same house edge in European Roulette

Bets to Avoid

  • Single number bets: Same house edge, but much higher variance (risk)
  • American Roulette (if available): Has 00, increasing house edge to 5.26%

Betting Systems (Warning!)

Many players use betting systems, but none can overcome the house edge:

  • Martingale: Double bet after each loss (dangerous - can lose everything quickly)
  • Fibonacci: Follow Fibonacci sequence (still loses long-term)
  • D'Alembert: Increase/decrease by one unit (doesn't work)

Reality: Roulette is a game of pure chance. No system can beat the house edge over time.

Smart Play

  • Stick to outside bets for lower variance
  • Set a budget and stick to it
  • Don't chase losses - each spin is independent
  • Play for entertainment, not to make money
  • Avoid betting systems - they don't work

üìö History

Origins

Roulette was invented in France in the 18th century. The name comes from the French word meaning "little wheel." Blaise Pascal is sometimes credited with creating an early version while trying to create a perpetual motion machine.

European vs American

  • European Roulette: Single zero (0) - 2.7% house edge
  • American Roulette: Double zero (0 and 00) - 5.26% house edge
  • European is better for players (lower house edge)

Cultural Impact

Roulette is one of the most iconic casino games, featured in:

  • Movies: Casablanca, The Sting, Run Lola Run
  • Literature: Dostoevsky's "The Gambler"
  • Music: "The Man Who Broke the Bank at Monte Carlo"

Famous Stories

  • Joseph Jagger (1873): Noticed wheel bias and won ¬£65,000
  • Charles Wells (1891): "The Man Who Broke the Bank at Monte Carlo" - won 1 million francs
  • Modern: Some players still look for biased wheels, but modern wheels are very precise

üí° Tips

  • Play European Roulette (single zero) when possible - better odds
  • Stick to outside bets for more consistent (but smaller) wins
  • Remember: Each spin is completely independent - past results don't matter
  • Set a budget before you start and stick to it
  • Don't use betting systems - they don't work and can lead to big losses
  • If you're ahead, consider cashing out - the house edge will catch up over time
  • Play for fun, not to make money - roulette is entertainment, not investment
  • Watch the wheel spin - it's part of the excitement!

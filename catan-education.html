History

Settlers of Catan (now simply called Catan) was designed by Klaus Teuber and first published in 1995 in Germany as Die Siedler von Catan. It revolutionized modern board gaming and is considered one of the most influential games of all time.

Inception

Klaus Teuber, a German dental technician, began designing board games in the 1980s. After winning three Spiel des Jahres awards for other games, he created Settlers of Catan in 1995. The game was inspired by his desire to create a game that combined strategy, luck, and social interaction.

The game's innovative features included:

  • Variable board setup: Hexagonal tiles arranged differently each game
  • Resource management: Collect and trade resources to build
  • Negotiation: Players must trade with each other
  • Accessible complexity: Easy to learn, strategic to master

Impact & Reception

  • 1995: Winner of Spiel des Jahres (Game of the Year)
  • 1995: Winner of Deutscher Spiele Preis (German Game Prize)
  • 1996: Winner of Origins Award for Best Fantasy or Science Fiction Board Game
  • Over 40 million copies sold worldwide
  • Translated into 40+ languages
  • Considered the "gateway game" that introduced millions to modern board gaming

Evolution

Since 1995, Catan has expanded into numerous editions and expansions:

  • Seafarers (1997) - Adds ships and exploration
  • Cities & Knights (1998) - Adds city improvements and barbarians
  • Traders & Barbarians (2007) - Multiple scenarios and variants
  • Explorers & Pirates (2013) - Exploration and pirate scenarios
  • Rise of the Inkas (2018) - South American theme
  • Star Trek Catan - Themed version
  • Game of Thrones Catan - Themed version
  • Plus many standalone games and digital versions

Cultural Impact

Settlers of Catan is credited with:

  • Popularizing modern board gaming in North America
  • Creating the "Eurogame" genre (strategy-focused, less luck-dependent)
  • Inspiring countless other games
  • Being featured in TV shows (The Big Bang Theory, Parks and Recreation)
  • Having a dedicated competitive scene

Game Rules

Objective

The goal is to be the first player to reach 10 Victory Points. Points are earned by:

  • Building settlements (1 VP each)
  • Building cities (2 VP each, upgrade from settlement)
  • Building the longest road (2 VP)
  • Having the largest army (2 VP)
  • Development cards (some give VP)

The Board

The island of Catan consists of 19 hexagonal tiles arranged in a specific pattern:

  • Row 1: 3 hexes
  • Row 2: 4 hexes
  • Row 3: 5 hexes (middle row)
  • Row 4: 4 hexes
  • Row 5: 3 hexes

Number Tokens

Each resource hex (except desert) has a number token (2-12, excluding 7). When the dice roll matches a number:

  • All players with settlements or cities adjacent to that hex collect resources
  • Settlements produce 1 resource
  • Cities produce 2 resources
  • Numbers 6 and 8 (red tokens) are most likely to be rolled
  • Rolling 7 activates the robber (see below)

Setup

  1. Place all 19 hex tiles in the standard layout
  2. Place number tokens on each hex (except desert)
  3. Each player places 2 settlements and 2 roads (in turns)
  4. Players collect resources from their starting settlements
  5. Place the robber on the desert hex

Gameplay

On your turn, you must do the following in order:

  1. Roll Dice:
    • Roll two dice
    • If 7: Move robber (see below)
    • Otherwise: All players collect resources from hexes with matching numbers
  2. Trade:
    • Trade with other players (negotiate)
    • OR trade 4:1 with the bank (any 4 of same resource for 1 of any resource)
    • OR use a 3:1 port if you have a settlement on one
  3. Build:
    • Build roads, settlements, cities, or buy development cards
    • You can build as much as you can afford

The Robber

When a 7 is rolled:

  1. All players with more than 7 cards must discard half (rounded down)
  2. The active player moves the robber to any hex
  3. That hex produces no resources while the robber is there
  4. The active player steals 1 card from a player with a settlement/city on that hex

Special Cards

  • Knight Card: Move robber, may help get largest army
  • Road Building: Build 2 roads for free
  • Year of Plenty: Take any 2 resources
  • Monopoly: Take all of one resource type from all players
  • Victory Point Card: Hidden VP (counts toward 10)

Winning

The first player to reach 10 Victory Points wins immediately. Points come from:

  • Settlements (1 VP each)
  • Cities (2 VP each)
  • Longest Road (2 VP)
  • Largest Army (2 VP)
  • Victory Point development cards (1 VP each)

Strategy & Tips

Initial Placement

  • Diversity: Place settlements on different resource types
  • High numbers: Prioritize hexes with 6 and 8 (most likely rolls)
  • Ports: Consider placing near ports for better trading
  • Expansion: Place where you can build roads to new areas
  • Block opponents: Place to limit opponent expansion

Resource Management

  • Balance: Try to have access to all 5 resources
  • Trade early: Don't hoard resources - trade to build
  • Watch the robber: Keep hand size under 8 cards when possible
  • Port strategy: Build on ports to get 2:1 trades
  • Development cards: Buy them early for flexibility

Building Strategy

  • Roads first: Build roads to expand and claim longest road
  • Settlements: Build settlements to get more resources
  • Cities: Upgrade settlements to cities for double resources
  • Development cards: Buy for knights (largest army) and VP cards
  • Timing: Build when you can, don't wait for perfect hands

Trading

  • Negotiate: Make fair trades with opponents
  • Block trades: Don't trade resources that help opponents win
  • Ports: Use 2:1 ports when you have excess of one resource
  • Bank trades: 4:1 bank trades are expensive but sometimes necessary
  • Trade timing: Trade before building, not after

Robber Strategy

  • Block opponents: Place robber on hexes opponents need
  • Steal strategically: Steal from players with many cards
  • Protect yourself: Keep hand size low to avoid discarding
  • Knight cards: Use knights to move robber when needed

Victory Point Strategy

  • Multiple paths: Pursue multiple VP sources (settlements, cities, longest road, largest army)
  • Hidden VPs: Buy development cards for hidden VP cards
  • Longest road: Build continuously to claim longest road
  • Largest army: Play knight cards to get largest army
  • Count points: Keep track of everyone's visible VPs

Tips for Beginners

  • Start with diversity: Place initial settlements on different resource types
  • Watch the numbers: Prioritize hexes with 6 and 8 tokens
  • Trade actively: Don't be afraid to trade with opponents
  • Build roads: Roads are cheap and help you expand
  • Upgrade to cities: Cities produce double resources
  • Buy development cards: They provide flexibility and hidden VPs
  • Watch hand size: Keep under 8 cards to avoid discarding on 7
  • Plan ahead: Think about where you want to expand
  • Block opponents: Use robber and placement to slow opponents
  • Have fun: Catan is about negotiation and strategy!

üìú The Rules

Objective: Score the most points by placing tiles, claiming features (roads, cities, monasteries, fields), and completing them.

Setup

  • Start with one tile face-up
  • Each player has meeples (followers) in their color
  • Draw tiles randomly

Turn Sequence

  1. Draw and place a tile: Must connect to existing tiles (edges must match)
  2. Optionally place a meeple: On the tile you just placed (road, city, monastery, or field)
  3. Score completed features: If you complete a road, city, or monastery, score it and get your meeple back

Scoring

  • Roads: 1 point per tile when completed
  • Cities: 2 points per tile (4 for large cities with pennants)
  • Monasteries: 9 points when surrounded by 8 tiles
  • Fields: Scored at game end, 3 points per completed city touching the field

üéØ Strategy Tips

Meeple Management

  • Don't waste meeples: You only have 7 - use them wisely
  • Quick returns: Place on features you can complete quickly
  • Save for fields: Fields score big at the end but tie up meeples

Tile Placement

  • Block opponents: Prevent them from completing their features
  • Steal features: Connect to opponent's incomplete features to share points
  • Create opportunities: Set up features you can claim later

Feature Control

  • Majority matters: Player with most meeples on a feature gets all points
  • Share strategically: Sometimes sharing is better than losing
  • Watch the board: Count remaining tiles to plan your moves

Game Design

The game's tile-laying mechanic creates a unique board every game. The simple rules but deep strategy have made it one of the most popular modern board games.

Expansions

Carcassonne has numerous expansions adding new tiles, mechanics, and features, keeping the game fresh and interesting for years of play.

Objective: Clear lines by filling horizontal rows completely. The game gets faster as you progress!

üéØ Strategy Tips

Basic Strategy

  • Keep it flat: Avoid creating peaks and valleys
  • Leave a well: Keep one column open for the I-piece (long bar)
  • Plan ahead: Think about where the next piece will fit
  • Clear from bottom: Don't leave gaps at the bottom

Advanced Techniques

  • T-Spins: Rotate T-pieces into tight spaces for bonus points
  • Stacking: Build high on one side, clear from the other
  • Combo building: Set up multiple line clears in a row
  • Speed management: At high speeds, prioritize survival over scoring

Piece Management

  • I-piece: Save for Tetris (4-line clear)
  • S and Z pieces: Dangerous - can create gaps
  • L and J pieces: Good for filling corners
  • T-piece: Versatile, great for T-spins
  • O-piece (square): Fits anywhere, use to fill gaps

üìö History

Tetris was created in 1984 by Russian game designer Alexey Pajitnov while working at the Soviet Academy of Sciences. The name comes from "tetra" (four) and "tennis" (Pajitnov's favorite sport).

Cultural Impact

Tetris became one of the best-selling video games of all time. It was bundled with the original Game Boy, helping make that console a massive success. The game has appeared on virtually every gaming platform.

World Records

Professional Tetris players can achieve incredible scores. The classic NES version world record exceeds 1.2 million points. Modern competitive Tetris uses different rules and scoring systems.

## The Chess Computation Problem

### Why "Mate in 2241" is Nonsense

**Chess Theory**:
- Starting position: Likely a draw (unsolved, but probable)
- **No forced mate**: Neither side can force checkmate with perfect play
- **Longest possible game**: ~5900 moves (with 50-move rule, threefold repetition)
- **"Mate in 2241"**: Mathematically absurd!

**Why Opponent Matters** (You're Right!):
- **Key Insight**: Opponent plays at THEIR level
- If opponent is also megabrain (perfect play) ‚Üí DRAW
- If opponent is human (imperfect) ‚Üí Eventually win, but not "mate in X"
- **Can't predict**: Depends on opponent's specific mistakes
- **Absurdity**: Announcing "mate in 2241" assumes opponent makes EXACT sequence of errors!

**The Joke**:
- Megabrain calculates "mate in 2241"
- **Assumes**: Opponent makes specific mistakes on moves 47, 203, 891, 1456...
- **Reality**: Opponent might play differently on move 2!
- **Absurdity**: Predicting 2241 moves of imperfect play is MORE impossible than solving chess!

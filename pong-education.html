The original video game

üìú The Rules

Objective: Hit the ball past your opponent's paddle to score. First to reach the target score wins!

How to Play

  • Use arrow keys (or W/S) to move your paddle up and down
  • The ball bounces off paddles and top/bottom walls
  • Score a point when the ball passes your opponent's paddle
  • First player to reach the target score (usually 11) wins

Controls

  • Player 1: W (up) / S (down) or Arrow Up / Arrow Down
  • Player 2: Arrow keys (if two-player mode)
  • AI Mode: Play against computer opponent

üéØ Strategy Tips

Basic Strategy

  • Angle control: Hit ball with different parts of paddle for different angles
  • Center hits: Return ball straight
  • Edge hits: Create sharp angles to surprise opponent
  • Positioning: Stay near center, move to intercept

Advanced Techniques

  • Spin: Moving paddle while hitting creates spin
  • Feinting: Move one way, then quickly change direction
  • Speed variation: Mix fast and slow returns
  • Corner shots: Aim for corners to make returns difficult

üìö History

Pong was created by Atari in 1972 and is considered the first commercially successful video game. It was inspired by table tennis.

Historical Significance

Pong launched the video game industry. It was simple, addictive, and proved that video games could be a commercial success. It's the grandfather of all video games!

Legacy

Pong has been recreated countless times and remains a classic. Modern versions include power-ups, multiple balls, and various game modes, but the core gameplay is timeless.

Don't get stuck with the Old Maid!

üìú The Rules

Objective: Avoid being left with the Old Maid card (the unmatched card). The player left with it loses!

Setup

  • Remove one queen from a standard 52-card deck (leaving 51 cards)
  • The remaining queen becomes the "Old Maid"
  • Deal all cards to players (some may have one extra card)

How to Play

  1. Discard pairs: All players remove matching pairs from their hand face-down
  2. Take turns: Players take turns offering their hand face-down to the player on their left
  3. Draw a card: The player draws one card (without looking) from the offered hand
  4. Make pairs: If the drawn card matches a card in hand, discard the pair
  5. Continue: Play continues until all pairs are discarded
  6. Loser: The player left with the Old Maid loses!

üéØ Strategy Tips

Basic Strategy

  • Memorize cards: Remember which cards you've seen
  • Watch opponents: Notice their reactions when you draw
  • Position matters: Being last to offer your hand can be advantageous
  • Bluff: Try not to react when someone draws a card you need

Psychological Play

  • Poker face: Don't show emotion when cards are drawn
  • Reverse psychology: Sometimes act disappointed when you get a good card
  • Timing: Try to get rid of the Old Maid when opponents aren't paying attention

üìö History

Old Maid is a traditional card game dating back centuries. It's a simple game of chance and memory, perfect for children and families.

Variations

Many variations exist worldwide. Some use jokers instead of removing a queen. The game is known by different names in different countries.

Educational Value

Old Maid helps children learn matching, memory, and social skills. It's a classic family game that requires no special equipment beyond a deck of cards.

The rummy variant with canastas

üìú The Rules

Objective: Score points by forming melds, especially canastas (7+ cards of the same rank).

How to Play

  • Start with 11 cards
  • Use 2 standard decks + 4 jokers (108 cards total)
  • On your turn, draw a card (from deck or discard pile)
  • Form melds (3+ cards of the same rank) and lay them down
  • Discard one card to end your turn
  • Go out by playing all cards in hand

Melds and Canastas

  • Meld: Three or more cards of the same rank (can include jokers)
  • Canasta: Seven or more cards of the same rank - worth 500 bonus points!
  • Jokers: Can be used as wild cards in any meld
  • All cards in a meld must be the same rank

Scoring

  • Canasta (7+ cards): 500 points
  • Regular meld (3-6 cards): 20 points per card
  • Going out: 100 bonus points

üéØ Strategy Tips

Canasta Strategy

  • Build toward canastas: Focus on collecting 7+ of the same rank
  • Use jokers wisely: Save jokers for canastas or high-value melds
  • Multiple canastas: Try to form several canastas for maximum points

Card Management

  • Keep pairs: Pairs are valuable - they can become melds
  • Watch the discard pile: See what's being discarded
  • Don't discard needed cards: Keep cards that might form canastas

Going Out

  • Plan your exit: Make sure you can go out before discarding
  • Canasta requirement: Some variants require a canasta to go out
  • Score calculation: Count points before going out

üìö History

Canasta was developed in Uruguay in the 1940s and became extremely popular in the United States in the 1950s. The name "canasta" means "basket" in Spanish.

Popularity

Canasta was a social phenomenon in the 1950s, especially among women's groups. It remains popular today, particularly in Latin America and among older players in the United States.

Variants

There are many Canasta variants including Hand and Foot, Samba, and Modern American Canasta, each with slightly different rules and scoring systems.

üí° Common Mistakes

  • Wasting jokers: Don't use jokers in small melds - save for canastas
  • Ignoring canastas: Canastas are worth huge points - prioritize them
  • Poor discard choices: Don't discard cards that could form canastas
  • Going out too early: Make sure you've maximized your score first

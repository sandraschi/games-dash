The classic meld and discard game

üìú The Rules

Objective: Be the first to form all your cards into valid melds and discard your last card.

How to Play

  • Start with 7 cards
  • On your turn, draw a card (from deck or discard pile)
  • Form melds (sets or runs) and lay them down
  • Discard one card to end your turn
  • First to play all cards wins!

  • Melds must have at least 3 cards
  • You can lay down multiple melds in one turn

üéØ Strategy Tips

Card Management

  • Keep flexible cards: Cards that can form multiple melds are valuable
  • Discard high cards: High cards are harder to use in runs
  • Watch the discard pile: See what opponent discards to guess their hand

Meld Strategy

  • Lay down early: Don't wait too long - get melds on the table
  • Multiple melds: Try to form several melds simultaneously
  • Extend melds: Add cards to existing melds when possible

Reading Opponents

  • Discard patterns: What they discard reveals what they're collecting
  • Pickup behavior: Taking from discard pile shows what they need
  • Blocking: Discard cards that might help opponent

üìö History

Rummy is a family of matching-card games. The basic concept dates back to the early 20th century, with origins possibly in Mexico or China.

Variants

There are many Rummy variants including Gin Rummy, Canasta, Kalooki, and Contract Rummy. Each has slightly different rules and scoring.

Popularity

Rummy is one of the most popular card games worldwide, especially in India where it's a cultural favorite. It's also popular in the United States and Europe.

üí° Common Mistakes

  • Holding too long: Don't wait for perfect melds - lay down what you can
  • Ignoring discard pile: The discard pile is valuable information
  • Poor discards: Don't discard cards that help opponent
  • Missing opportunities: Look for ways to extend existing melds

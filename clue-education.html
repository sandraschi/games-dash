üìú The Rules

Objective: Be the first to correctly guess who committed the murder, with what weapon, and in which room.

Setup

  • One suspect, weapon, and room card are placed in the "Case File" (secret)
  • Remaining cards are dealt to players
  • Players move around the board to enter rooms

Gameplay

  • Roll dice and move your character
  • When in a room, make a suggestion (suspect + weapon + current room)
  • Other players must show you ONE card from your suggestion if they have it
  • Take notes to track what you've learned
  • When ready, make an accusation (final guess)
  • If correct, you win! If wrong, you're out of the game

üéØ Strategy Tips

Note-Taking

  • Track everything: Write down every card you see
  • Process of elimination: If no one shows a card, it might be in the case file
  • Watch reactions: See which players can't show cards

Suggestions

  • Use your own cards: Suggest cards you already have to see others' cards
  • Test systematically: Try different combinations methodically
  • Move suspects: Suggestions move suspect pieces - use strategically

Accusations

  • Wait until certain: Only accuse when you're confident
  • Check your notes: Make sure you've eliminated all other possibilities
  • Timing matters: Accuse before opponents figure it out

üìö History

Clue (originally "Cluedo" in the UK) was invented in 1943 by Anthony E. Pratt, a British musician. The game was inspired by murder mystery parties popular in the 1940s.

Original Characters

The classic suspects: Colonel Mustard, Professor Plum, Miss Scarlet, Mr. Green, Mrs. White, and Mrs. Peacock. The game has been adapted into movies, TV shows, and video games.

Cultural Impact

Clue has become one of the most popular board games of all time, with numerous editions, spin-offs, and a cult classic 1985 film adaptation with multiple endings.
